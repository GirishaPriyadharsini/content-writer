write first, cut later (even if it's messy)

Most beginners open editing software and immediately start trimming clips. I’ve been there — but the real shift happened when I started scripting my emotions before editing. Grab your phone notes, write a few lines: “here the music drops — I want the viewer to feel lonely — then cut to hands.” That’s video content writing. It guides your cuts like a compass. And the best part? Alight Motion and CapCut are all I need to bring that script to life.

✍️ Beginner exercise: pick any 10-second clip. write a 30-word internal monologue. then edit the footage to match the rhythm of your words using only free apps. You’ll be shocked how cinematic it feels.

In the fan edit community, this is called "POV scripting" — you're not just cutting footage, you're directing a feeling. My square edit "lost letters" started as a 4-line journal entry. The wide cinematic piece "golden hour drift" was built from a voice note I recorded while walking. Alight Motion for keyframes + CapCut for transitions = my whole universe right now. And when I eventually save up for a PC, I'll be excited to learn DaVinci Resolve — but that doesn't make what I make today any less valuable. Every tool teaches you storytelling. one fan edit = one complete story

I specialize in fan edits that don't need explanations. You watch once, and you feel the entire arc — beginning, middle, end. That’s because I script each edit like a mini film: a question, a conflict, a quiet resolution. No bloated runtime, just raw emotion. People in my comments say things like “how is this only 50 seconds?” That's the power of writing before cutting.

My most viral fan edit (200k+ views) had a script no longer than 80 words. The voiceover was recorded on my phone mic. The edit? Simple jump cuts in CapCut and a few keyframes in Alight Motion. Tools don't matter — intention does. I can explain a whole story with a single edit. And when I finally build a PC, I'll be learning DaVinci Resolve to add color grading and Fusion compositing to my toolkit. No shame in growth. No shame in starting small. Let's all evolve together.

Whether it's 1:1 square for reels or 16:9 widescreen for YouTube, the process stays the same: feel first, write second, cut third on mobile. Your timeline doesn't know if you're using Premiere or CapCut — it only knows rhythm and heart. And honestly? Alight Motion gives me more control than I ever needed. But one day I'll dive into DaVinci's node-based grading, and that will be a new adventure, not a betrayal of my roots.
